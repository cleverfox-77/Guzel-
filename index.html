"I instinctively look to the rich architecture of my country. The iconic Ahsan Manzil has become a signature motif the brand is widely recognised by."

Founded by Nafiz Imtiaz Chowdhury at 18, Guzel emerged from a simple frustration — that Bangladeshi fashion designers who genuinely invested in their craft and broke from Western influence were almost non-existent. He decided to become one.

— Nafiz Imtiaz Chowdhury, Founder & Designer, Banani Atelier

The Signature

THE PINK
PALACE
LIVES
IN EVERY
STITCH

Nafiz grew up painting, obsessed with the arched corridors, ornate facades, and terracotta warmth of Bangladesh's Mughal and colonial architecture. The Ahsan Manzil — Old Dhaka's iconic riverside palace — became his obsession and his canvas.

Every arch, every cornice, every colour from that building finds its way into what we create. It's not decoration — it's identity.
Read the Story

HOW WE
CREATE

01
SKETCH

Every collection begins with Nafiz's hand — pencil on paper, colour studies, architectural references from Old Dhaka's streets. No digital shortcuts at this stage.

02
SOURCE

Fabrics hand-selected bolt-by-bolt from Islampur — Dhaka's legendary textile bazaar. Muslin, jamdani, fine kurta silks chosen by touch and eye alone.

03
CRAFT

All production — prints, embroidery, cutting, sewing, tailoring — happens entirely in-house at our Banani atelier. Every karigar is a permanent member of the Guzel family.

04
DELIVER

Each finished piece is an individual. Bespoke made-to-measure or exclusive ready-to-wear — curated to the person who'll carry it into the world.

The Atelier

EVERYTHING
IN-HOUSE.
NOTHING
OUTSOURCED.

Since 2019, every single Guzel garment — from the block prints and embroidery to the final seam — is made within our Banani atelier. Nafiz personally oversees each production stage to guarantee a quality and individuality that no external factory can replicate.

Our karigar artisans aren't contractors. They are permanent, skilled members of the Guzel family — their expertise, their stories, and their craft form the soul of every piece that leaves our doors.
